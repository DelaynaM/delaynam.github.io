Chapter One: A Place I Recognize

The note in the manuscript said: "They are erasing pages. Read carefully."

I didn’t understand what it meant — until I recognized a name scrawled in the margin: Bellamy Street. It’s real. It’s local. It used to be a bookstore that burned down when I was a kid.

I went to look. The building is still there… but the burn marks are gone. The boarded windows are clean. And taped to the door was a fresh piece of paper, typed on the same kind of machine as the manuscript:

“Whatever you think you know, you don't.”

Bellamy wasn't the only street.
